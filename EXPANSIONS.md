# What EGAP Stands For

**EGAP** is the permanent name. The acronym is the brand. The expansion you encounter depends on where you stand in the ecosystem.

All expansions describe the same protocol. The semantic anchors — **Governance**, **Agents**, **Protocol** — never change. Only the emphasis changes, to meet you where you are.

---

## The Six Canonical Expansions

### 1. Engine Governed Agents Protocol

**Audience:** Engineers, architects, SREs, platform teams.

**Where you'll see it:** The formal specification, GitHub repositories, technical documentation, SDK reference, RFC-style material.

**What it emphasises:** The architectural relationship between an orchestration Engine and the Agents it dispatches. The Engine governs. The Agents execute. Governance is structural, not advisory.

---

### 2. Enterprise Governance for Agents Protocol

**Audience:** CIOs, CISOs, CTOs, heads of platform engineering, enterprise procurement.

**Where you'll see it:** Enterprise sales collateral, RFP responses, analyst briefings, enterprise-facing landing pages.

**What it emphasises:** EGAP as the open standard for managing AI agents with identity, authorization, audit, approvals, and alerts at production scale. Aligned with the vocabulary of enterprise governance, compliance frameworks, and procurement requirements.

---

### 3. Ethical Governance for Agentic Platforms

**Audience:** AI ethics boards, academic AI safety researchers, policy think tanks, responsible-AI committees.

**Where you'll see it:** Whitepapers, academic conference talks (FAccT, AIES, Partnership on AI), responsible-AI policy papers.

**What it emphasises:** EGAP as the technical substrate that makes human-in-the-loop AI deployment practical at scale. Approval gates, immutable audit, budget bounds — the concrete mechanisms behind abstract ethical commitments.

---

### 4. Elevated Governance of Autonomous Processes

**Audience:** Operations leaders, DevOps executives, SRE directors, digital transformation teams.

**Where you'll see it:** DevOps and SRE conferences, platform engineering community talks, operational maturity narratives.

**What it emphasises:** EGAP as a maturity step — the discipline that lets autonomous systems run in production with accountability. Broadens the scope from AI agents to any autonomous execution that needs governance.

---

### 5. Every Governed Agent Protocol

**Audience:** The open-source community, standards bodies, CNCF audiences, universal-adoption messaging.

**Where you'll see it:** CNCF sandbox materials, community launch campaigns, OSS conferences, GitHub org profiles.

**What it emphasises:** Radical inclusivity. Every agent, everywhere, governed. Positions EGAP as universal infrastructure — not enterprise-vertical, not region-specific, not vendor-controlled.

---

### 6. Evidence-based Governance for AI Protocols

**Audience:** Regulators, central banks, RBI, IDRBT, MAS, SEC, ESMA, BIS, IMF-style policy bodies.

**Where you'll see it:** Regulatory submissions, financial stability reports, sovereign AI policy documents, central bank working papers.

**What it emphasises:** Verifiable outcomes. Immutable audit trails. Cryptographic approval records. Conformance testing. Everything regulators need to treat an AI system as auditable rather than opaque.

---

## Why Multiple Expansions

Most protocols pick one audience and let the name reflect that audience. The cost: the protocol is harder to adopt outside its originating community.

EGAP is deliberately designed to serve multiple audiences at once. The acronym stays fixed — the same four letters across every conversation — but the expansion flexes to match the room.

This is common practice for widely-adopted acronyms:

- **HTTP** is "HyperText Transfer Protocol" in specifications, "the web protocol" in everyday speech.
- **SQL** is "Structured Query Language" academically, "database language" conversationally.
- **REST** is "Representational State Transfer" formally, "API style" in practice.

The acronym compounds brand recognition. The expansion carries positioning.

---

## What Does Not Change

Regardless of expansion:

- **EGAP is one protocol with one specification.** The wire format, message types, governance metadata, approval flow, audit schema, and conformance criteria are identical across all audiences.

- **EGAP is one brand.** The acronym, the logo, the trademark, the domain (egaprotocol.org), and the GitHub organisation are singular and permanent.

- **EGAP is one standard.** Implementations either pass the conformance test suite or they do not. There is no "enterprise edition" of the protocol that differs from the "community edition."

- **EGAP has one governance model.** The project is maintained under the governance described in [GOVERNANCE.md](./GOVERNANCE.md), regardless of how any given audience encounters the name.

The expansions are positioning tools. The protocol is singular.

## Using the Expansions

### For contributors and implementers

Use **Engine Governed Agents Protocol** in technical documentation, code comments, API reference material, and formal specifications. This is the primary expansion for engineering work and maps directly to the Engine and Agent roles defined in the spec.

### For enterprise adopters and decision-makers

Use **Enterprise Governance for Agents Protocol** in procurement documents, vendor evaluations, architecture decision records, and board-level reporting on AI governance posture.

### For regulators and policy audiences

Use **Evidence-based Governance for AI Protocols** in regulatory filings, central bank submissions, and sovereign AI policy documents.

### For community and open-source audiences

Use **Every Governed Agent Protocol** in CNCF engagement, OSS launch materials, and community-building messaging.

### For ethics and research audiences

Use **Ethical Governance for Agentic Platforms** in academic papers, AI safety venues, and responsible-AI publications.

### For operations and platform audiences

Use **Elevated Governance of Autonomous Processes** in DevOps, SRE, and platform-maturity contexts.

### When in doubt

**Just use EGAP.** The acronym carries the meaning. Readers familiar with the protocol will know what it is. Readers unfamiliar can follow any of the six expansions to the same spec.

## Additional Expansions

The six canonical expansions above are the officially recognised forms. Additional descriptive expansions may be introduced over time as EGAP enters new verticals or regulatory contexts. Any new expansion MUST:

- Preserve the core semantic anchors: **Governance**, **Agents** (or closely related agentic/autonomous concept), **Protocol**.
- Describe the same underlying specification without suggesting a variant protocol.
- Be approved by the Technical Steering Committee per the process in [GOVERNANCE.md](./GOVERNANCE.md).

Candidate expansions under consideration include Explainable Governance for AI Platforms, Embedded Governance in Agentic Platforms, and Empirical Governance of AI Processes.
